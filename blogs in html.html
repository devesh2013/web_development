What School Supplies Do I Need for 1st Grade?

If your child is heading into first grade, you might be wondering: What school supplies do I need for 1st grade? From waterproof backpacks to the right reading books, we’ve got you covered with a smart, parent-friendly checklist to help you feel confident, organized, and ready for the year ahead. First, Know What to Expect in 1st Grade First grade is when foundational skills really start to take shape. Your child will be diving into more structured reading and writing, building basic math fluency, and gaining more independence. Here are a few first grade expectations you can generally count on: Reading short stories and beginning chapter books Learning to write full sentences and simple paragraphs Exploring addition and subtraction within 20 Building skills in science, social studies, and art Participating in group work and developing social-emotional skills Being ready for first grade isn’t just about academics — it’s also about showing up organized, focused, and prepared with the right tools.


The Ultimate 1st Grade School Supply List

Here’s a solid baseline of first grade supplies most schools request: Backpack (preferably waterproof) – Look for something lightweight but roomy enough to carry folders, books, and lunch. A waterproof backpack is ideal for unpredictable weather and water bottle spills. Lunchbox + water bottle – A leak-proof, easy-to-clean set is a must. Labeled folders – One for homework, one for classwork, and maybe one for “keep at home” papers. Composition notebooks or primary-ruled notebooks – These are ideal for early writing and journaling. #2 pencils + erasers – Go with pre-sharpened packs if possible (your kid’s teacher will thank you). Crayons, markers, and colored pencils – A small set of each goes a long way for art projects and classroom work. Glue sticks and child-safe scissors – Essential for hands-on activities. Dry erase markers – Often used for group work and whiteboard practice. Pencil case – Keeps things from getting lost in the backpack jungle. Headphones or earbuds (with a case!) – Some schools require these for computer lab time or tablet use. Should I Label My Kid’s School Supplies?

Short answer: YES. Labeling your child’s supplies helps prevent confusion, mix-ups, and lost items. You don’t have to go overboard — a simple waterproof name label or even permanent marker initials will do the trick. Especially for things like water bottles, headphones, lunchboxes, and jackets, labeling is one of those small steps that can save a lot of headaches later
